Chopin’s Last Manuscript

A lyrical and affecting story that elegantly re-creates the world, and the wounds, of a musical legend.

From the salons of 1830s Paris to the solitude of Mallorca, this novel explores passion, betrayal, and the hidden truths behind Chopin’s final days.

Elizabeth Allan Kellam

Beth has had many lives — a classical music student, a military wife, a corporate marketing researcher, a founder of two businesses, and now a writer living in Cape Cod and St. Augustine.

She has long been captivated by the hidden truths in music history. Chopin’s Last Manuscript is her debut novel, the first in a planned series exploring the lives and artistic struggles of legendary composers and musicians.

Beth believes that “writing about music is like painting in black and white.” You must hear it to feel the color, to connect to the soul of the musician.

She has created a new concept known as immersive reading experiences. The ebook allows readers to listen to music while reading through embedded links, while print editions provide access to curated soundtracks.
